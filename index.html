02. MOTIVATION

The Economic Imperative: Taiwan's Challenge

Taiwan has one of the highest prevalence rates of End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD) globally. Poorly managed Diabetes Mellitus is a primary driver.

Policy Implication: CGM-enabled telemonitoring can improve Glycemic Control (HbA1c) and Time-In-Range (TIR), directly reducing Diabetic Nephropathy and saving billions in future dialysis costs.

#1 Global Prevalence

Taiwan ranks top globally for dialysis prevalence.

The CGM Solution

Better control = Delayed complications = Cost savings.

05. THE PROPOSAL

The Triad Model

Closing the gap requires not more advanced devices but a redesigned care-delivery system — continuous glucose data flowing through an AI triage layer into structured clinical workflows. (Proposed as a model requiring confirmatory research.)

CGM Telemonitoring

Continuous, automatic glucose data.

Multidisciplinary Care

Coordinated clinical teams and workflows.

AI Decision Support

Triage and personalised guidance.

06. POLICY

Three Levels of Health-System Action

A policy-focused synthesis with specific implications for Taiwan's National Health Insurance (NHI) and comparable publicly funded systems.

Macro

Reimbursement reform and risk-stratified financing; digital infrastructure and AI governance.

Meso

Multidisciplinary care design and workforce preparation.

Micro

Patient behavioural support and health-equity monitoring.

Reimbursement Priority Zone

NHI currently reimburses CGM only for Type 1 diabetes. The largest, most cost-effective gains come from high-risk, non-insulin Type 2 patients (baseline HbA1c ≥ 8.0%) — the priority group for expanding reimbursement.
